<p><p>Functional Near Infrared Optical Brain Imaging System (fNIRS) utilizes near-infrared light to assess brain activity by measuring hemodynamic responses. Market types include FT-NIR spectroscopy, which employs Fourier Transform techniques for high-resolution spectral data, and dispersive NIR spectroscopy, which separates light into its components using prisms or gratings for analysis. Other methods, such as Acousto-Optic Tunable Filters (AOTF) and filter-based systems, offer alternative filtering techniques to isolate specific wavelengths, enhancing the versatility and applications of fNIRS technology in neuroscience.</p></p>
