<p><p>The commercial steer axle market comprises two primary types: driven steer axles and non-driven steer axles. Driven steer axles are powered, providing enhanced traction and control, particularly in challenging conditions, making them suitable for heavy-duty vehicles. In contrast, non-driven steer axles do not receive power from the engine, offering a lighter weight and reduced complexity, commonly found in lighter commercial trucks. Both types play critical roles in vehicle handling, stability, and overall performance in commercial transportation.</p></p>
